# G1 -> G2 -> 1M Steps: Milestone and Acceptance Criteria

**Date**: 2026-03-02
**Source**: Synthesis of DeepThink Architectural Audit & Project State

Having successfully implemented all P1-P3 architectural hardening requirements (Hard Interrupts, Ephemeral Workspaces, Micro-Snapshots) and mathematically fortified the engine (GBNF FSM Logit-Masking, Merkle Tree Sliding Window, Deterministic Temperature Dithering, and strict state-tape rollback without contextual leaks), TuringOS is officially transitioning out of the **G0 Stability Seed** phase.

We now strictly adhere to the following phase-gate definitions and acceptance criteria before moving to the next scale.

## Phase 1: G1 (Deterministic Hardening & Scale Readiness)
**Objective**: Empirically prove that moving from "probabilistic soft-prompting" to "mathematical constraints" (GBNF, Merkle Trees) permanently severs the $O(c^N)$ error-compounding curve that causes long-horizon LLM agents to collapse.

**Acceptance Criteria (Hard Gates)**:
1. **200 Consecutive Passes**: The baseline background daemon must reach and sustain over 200 consecutive benchmark passes without hanging.
2. **0% Format Hallucination**: The occurrence of `[oracle] repair parse failed` in the daemon logs (indicating JSON schema drift or parsing failure) must be strictly `0%`. The GBNF state machine must absolutely guarantee structural validity.
3. **Resilient Self-Healing**: When logic errors, loop attractors, or systemic traps occur, the engine must flawlessly trigger Red Flags, execute Merkle-backed resets, or physically rollback to $S_{t-1}$ via micro-snapshots without accumulating conversational "tape creep".

## Phase 2: G2 (Empirical Proof of Axiom 2 - Ensemble Lift)
**Objective**: Mathematically prove Condorcet's Jury Theorem within the LLM swarm. We must demonstrate that a 32B Planner acting as an Asymmetric Discriminator can route signal out of the noise generated by a high-entropy 7B Worker swarm.

**Acceptance Criteria (Hard Gates)**:
1. **Temperature Dithering Active**: The system must utilize the implemented deterministic PID-based temperature spreading ($T=0.1$ to $T=0.7$) to force cognitive diversity ($H > 0$) across the $K=16$ workers.
2. **Ablation Validation**: Execute a K-sweep benchmark ($K=1, 4, 8, 16$) and log the metrics in `src/bench/million-baseline-compare.ts`.
   - Measure $W_{acc}$ (Single 7B worker accuracy).
   - Measure $E_{oracle}$ (Upper bound: Did at least 1 of 16 workers generate the right transition?).
   - Measure $P_{lift}$ (Routing Efficiency: Did the Planner pick the right answer when it was in the minority?).
3. **The Proof**: The total system Pass Rate at $K=16$ must be statistically and significantly higher than at $K=1$.

## Phase 3: The 100-Worker Swarm (Tail Latency & 1M Steps)
**Objective**: Scale the swarm horizontally across the Tailscale VPN to $K=100$ and push towards the 1,000,000 step horizon.

**Acceptance Criteria (Hard Gates)**:
1. **P85 Quorum Yield Barrier**: Replace synchronous `Promise.all` waiting with a `Promise.any` wrapper. The orchestrator must aggressively advance as soon as the fastest 85% of workers reply, brutally dropping the remaining 15% to completely eliminate network tail-latency deadlock (Amdahl's Law).
2. **Sustained Scale**: Execute stable multi-hour runs at 100-concurrency without localized GPU lockups or cascading TCP timeouts affecting the controller loop.

*No Python watchdog or supervisor processes will be used to monitor this workflow.*
